TOUCHPOINTS IN SHINJUKU
Lost in Shinjuku
Shinjuku station
After several solo trips to Tokyo, Shinjuku still feels like another world. I was completely lost inside the station for nearly an hour on my first visit—but that chaos, where tourists, students, and office workers collide, is exactly what makes it so captivating.

Japanese Summer
Shinjuku Kyoen
Not long after watching Shunji Iwai’s All About Lily Chou-Chou, I came here alone one summer and immediately understood what a Japanese summer truly feels like. If you ever want a break from Shinjuku’s hectic cityscape, I recommend a quiet picnic with coffee at Shinjuku Gyoen—it’s the perfect escape.
The best brunch cafe
Aaliya Coffee
I’m not usually into desserts or brunch, but if someone asks me for a food recommendation in Tokyo, this place always comes to mind. Aaliya Coffee, with its retro 90s-style interior and nostalgic charm, makes you feel like you've slipped back in time. Drop by around noon for their French toast and a cup of coffee—it somehow feels like the start of a really good day.
Toho Cinema and Toyoko Kids
Kabukicho
Right next to Toho Cinemas in Shinjuku, a group of young people known as the Toyoko Kids has drawn public attention. Many of them are runaways or come from unstable home environments, gathering in this area with nowhere else to go. As an outsider, I didn’t fully understand the situation, but seeing them there made me feel a bit unsettled.
