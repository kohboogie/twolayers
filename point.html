TOUCHPOINTS IN SHIBUYA
My personal view
Shibuya Scramble
Shibuya might just be the most stylish part of Tokyo—and without it, the city feels incomplete. It’s where youth gathers, fashion pulses, and every corner feels like a page out of a street-style magazine. Just walking through its streets, you’re constantly hit with visuals that spark inspiration. It’s chaotic, beautiful, and impossible not to fall in love with.
Furugiya
Shibuya Vintage shops
Walk from Shibuya toward Harajuku, and you’ll find yourself in vintage heaven. In Japan, these shops are called furugiya—and they’re everywhere. What makes them special isn’t just the sheer number, but the way vintage is valued here. The clothes are thoughtfully curated, often in great condition, and sold at surprisingly reasonable prices. It’s the kind of place where you can stumble upon a one-of-a-kind piece and feel like you’ve uncovered a secret.
Relaxing places right under my nose!
Ebisu Station
If you’re feeling overwhelmed by the crowds and long lines at Shibuya’s cafes and restaurants, take a short trip to nearby Ebisu Station. Just a few minutes away, you'll find quieter streets, stylish shops, and a more laid-back, local atmosphere. It’s the kind of place where you can slow down and breathe a little.
Overtourism in Shibuya
Shibuya
In recent years, Japan has been grappling with the effects of overtourism—rising rent in once-local neighborhoods, overcrowded public transit, and a growing sense of fatigue among residents. Shibuya, with its global appeal, is one of the areas most visibly affected. As a traveler, I can’t help but feel both drawn to its charm and concerned about what might be lost along the way. .
